Research overview

Supporting teachers when implementation decisions happen quickly.

Mission: Reinforceable is part of dissertation research exploring how brief, game-based practice may support teacher implementation of individualized behavior support plans.

Play the Beta Beta Testing Details

Research purpose

The project focuses on teacher-friendly implementation support: practical prompts, realistic decisions, and immediate feedback that can fit into school routines.

Why teacher implementation support matters

Behavior support plans depend on consistent adult responses across real classroom routines. Teachers may understand the plan but still need low-burden opportunities to practice what to do during fast-moving moments.

What the tool is designed to support

The beta is designed to support plan-aligned decision-making, attention to the likely function of behavior, and reflection on feedback after each choice.

Study design overview

The current public beta supports usability and design refinement. Future study materials may describe participant procedures, practice routines, and feedback collection in more detail.

What data may be collected

Depending on the study phase, data may include usability feedback, teacher reflections, navigation issues, clarity ratings, and game interaction records. Any formal data collection should be reviewed through the relevant consent and study procedures.

Teacher experience

Teachers should experience the beta as brief, clear, and respectful of classroom realities. The goal is practice and feedback, not evaluation of teacher worth or professional skill.
