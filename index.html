The problem

Implementation support has to fit real classrooms.

Teachers are often asked to implement individualized behavior support plans in fast-moving classrooms. Even strong plans can be hard to carry out consistently when decisions need to happen quickly, privately, and with many other classroom demands in motion.

Mission: Reinforceable is designed around low-burden practice: short decision points, realistic scenarios, and feedback that connects choices back to behavior support plan implementation.

What it is

A web-based practice tool for behavior support plan implementation.

Mission: Reinforceable presents brief missions based on classroom scenarios. Teachers practice selecting responses, receive feedback, and can revisit progress over time.

1

Read the classroom scenario

Each mission starts with a short classroom situation connected to behavior support plan implementation.

2

Choose a response

Teachers choose from realistic options that vary in alignment with the plan and the likely function of behavior.

3

Get immediate feedback

Feedback explains why a response may support, weaken, or partially align with the intended plan.

Why it matters

Practice that respects teacher time.

Low-burden daily practice

Short missions are intended to fit into small windows of time.

Realistic decision-making

Scenarios focus on moments that require quick, plan-aligned choices.

Immediate feedback

Teachers see feedback right after responding, while the scenario is still fresh.

Implementation fidelity

The tool is designed to support consistent use of behavior support plan strategies.

Classroom fit

The experience is brief, web-based, and built around practical classroom routines.

Research and pilot

Part of dissertation research on teacher implementation support.

This project is part of dissertation research focused on supporting teacher implementation of behavior support plans. The current beta is being used to refine usability, clarity, navigation, and the overall fit of game-based practice for educators.

Pilot feedback and usability findings will be summarized here as they become available. No effectiveness claims are being made from the public beta page.
