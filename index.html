Mission: Reinforceable

Game-based practice for behavior support decisions.

Mission: Reinforceable helps teachers rehearse plan-aligned responses through brief, realistic classroom missions with immediate feedback before similar moments happen during the school day.

Why it exists

The gap is not the plan. It is the moment of implementation.

Behavior intervention/support plans can be carefully designed and still be difficult to use consistently in a busy classroom. Teachers are making quick decisions while managing instruction, transitions, peer interactions, and competing student needs.

Mission: Reinforceable was built for that moment: the space between knowing what the plan says and using it when it matters.

Practice loop

A five-minute practice loop before the routine begins.

Prompt

A daily email cue brings the behavior plan back into focus.

Practice

The teacher works through a short classroom scenario.

Feedback

The game explains how the choice aligns with the plan.

Plan-specific practice

Built for individualized behavior plans, not generic advice.

Each mission is designed around the student’s behavior plan, target routine, and key implementation steps. Teachers choose among responses that are plan-aligned, supportive but incomplete, or contraindicated, then receive feedback tied to the plan.

Pilot feedback

Pilot-tested with general education teachers.

6 general education teachers tested the beta version
93/100 average usability score
Strong feasibility and acceptability ratings across measured areas
Long-term use all teachers who completed final measures said they would use it long term

Pilot findings reflect usability, feasibility, and acceptability feedback. They should not be interpreted as evidence of effectiveness.

Dissertation research

Designed for dissertation research, refined for classroom use.

The dissertation study will evaluate whether adding Mission: Reinforceable to existing coaching-as-usual is associated with changes in teacher implementation fidelity, student behavior, maintenance, and teacher perceptions of classroom fit.
