Beta Briefing

Before you enter the beta, here is what you are testing, what the game is pretending to simulate, and what to watch for as you play.

Briefing 1

What this game is

Mission: Reinforceable is a game-based practice tool for teachers using behavior support plans. In the game, you step into the role of a teacher working through short classroom moments. You read what is happening, choose how to respond, and get feedback about whether that choice fits the student’s plan.

A behavior support plan is a written plan that helps adults respond consistently when a student has behavior that interferes with learning. It usually explains what triggers the behavior, what the student is trying to get or avoid, what skill the student should use instead, and how adults should respond.

Briefing 2

What this will look like for real teachers

In the dissertation version, each teacher’s game will be unique to their own classroom. The missions will be built around a specific student, a specific classroom routine, and the student’s actual behavior intervention/support plan.

Before a mission begins, the game gives the teacher a short BIP/BSP Briefing. That briefing explains the classroom situation, the student’s target behavior, the replacement skill or support strategy, and the key plan steps the teacher should be thinking about during the mission.

The goal is not to replace coaching or professional judgment. The goal is to give teachers a quick, low-risk practice space before similar moments happen during the school day.

Briefing 3

What beta testers should do

For this beta, you are not expected to be a behavior expert. You are testing the game experience itself. As you play, notice whether the setup makes sense, whether the choices are clickable and readable, whether the feedback is clear, and whether the game feels smooth enough to use.

  • What feels clear?
  • What feels confusing?
  • What feels fun or engaging?
  • What feels clunky, broken, or too hard to navigate?
  • Did the BIP Briefing give you enough context to make a choice?

Your feedback will help improve the game before it is used in the dissertation study.

Ready to enter the beta?
